What is YAKAP?

YAKAP stands for Yaman ng Kalusugan Program — PhilHealth's national primary care benefit package, launched in July 2025 as an upgraded replacement of the former Konsulta program. It gives all PhilHealth members and their qualified dependents free access to primary care services at accredited clinics nationwide.

YAKAP bundles four key benefits: free consultations (KONSULTA), access to 75 essential medicines worth up to ₱20,000 per year (GAMOT), 13 outpatient laboratory tests (LABORATORYO), and cancer screenings (SCREEN). The goal is to shift the focus from treating illness to preventing it — catching health problems early before they require hospitalization.

To use YAKAP, a PhilHealth member picks an accredited clinic, completes a First Patient Encounter (FPE) with a doctor, and gets enrolled. Registration can be done via the eGov PH app, the PhilHealth member portal, or directly at a YAKAP clinic.

Who benefits most?

💊
PhilHealth Members
All members and their declared dependents are eligible — covering the vast majority of Filipinos. No out-of-pocket cost for covered services.
🤱
Mothers & Newborns
Prenatal check-ups, postnatal care, and newborn health monitoring are core services at YAKAP-accredited health centers.
👴
Seniors & Chronic Patients
Regular monitoring for hypertension, diabetes, and other conditions, plus free maintenance medicines under the GAMOT program.
🔬
Those Needing Screening
Cancer screenings (mammogram, colonoscopy, liver ultrasound, CT scan) and lab tests like CBC, lipid profile, and fasting blood sugar are covered.
🏘️
Rural Communities
Many YAKAP centers are Rural Health Units (RHUs) serving as the primary — and often only — healthcare access point in remote areas.
👷
Workers & Informal Sector
YAKAP was specifically designed to make essential care accessible for workers and underserved communities who cannot easily afford hospital visits.
